// ABOUT ENGLAND

A Nation Woven From Centuries of Story

England is a country that has shaped the modern world in more ways than any other. Its literature, music, science, and law have left marks on every continent. From the Magna Carta to the Industrial Revolution, from Shakespeare to The Rolling Stones — England's cultural output is simply unmatched.

Yet beneath the global icons lies a nation of incredible local diversity: ancient dialects, regional festivals, stone-walled villages, and pub traditions that date back centuries.

// DID YOU KNOW?

Untold Fun Facts

Many people mistakenly believe that the iconic clock tower in London is named Big Ben. In fact, its official name is the Elizabeth Tower. The name "Big Ben" is actually the nickname for the giant 13.7-ton bell housed deep inside the tower!

The British are absolutely obsessed with tea! Collectively, they drink about 100 to 165 million cups of tea every single day. If you gathered all those cups over a year, it would be enough to fill roughly 20 Olympic-sized swimming pools.

There is an ancient superstition dating back to the reign of King Charles II stating that at least 6 ravens must reside at the Tower of London at all times. Legend has it that if these birds ever leave the tower, the British Kingdom will fall. To this day, the monarchy still employs a dedicated Ravenmaster to care for them.
