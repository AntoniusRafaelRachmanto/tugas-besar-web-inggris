A TASTE OF HERITAGE

The Culinary Art of Britain.

From cozy fire-lit country pubs to grand historic banquets, discover the hearty, comforting flavors and timeless culinary traditions that define classic English gastronomy.

Starters & Pub Bites

01

Sausage Rolls

A beloved staple found all across the UK. Deliciously seasoned minced pork sausage meat enveloped in layers of rich, flaky, and buttery puff pastry, baked until beautifully golden-brown.

02

Scotch Eggs

A quintessential British pub snack consisting of a perfectly hard-boiled or soft-boiled egg wrapped in savory seasoned sausage meat, coated in golden, crispy breadcrumbs, and deep-fried to perfection.

03

Prawn Cocktail

A retro British starter made with tender shelled prawns coated in a creamy, tangy Marie Rose sauce, beautifully arranged over a bed of crisp shredded lettuce and garnished with a pinch of paprika and a lemon wedge.

Traditional Main Courses

04

Full English Breakfast

A legendary, hearty platter featuring fried or poached eggs, sizzling back bacon, traditional pork sausages, grilled tomatoes, sautéed mushrooms, black pudding, baked beans, and buttery fried bread or toast.

05

Fish & Chips

The ultimate symbol of British seaside comfort food. Fresh, tender cod or haddock dipped in a light, crispy beer-batter, deep-fried and served alongside thick-cut golden chips, mushy peas, and tartar sauce.

06

The Sunday Roast

A historic weekly national ritual. Features succulent roasted meat (beef, lamb, or chicken) served with crispy roasted potatoes, assorted seasonal vegetables, rich meat gravy, and iconic fluffy Yorkshire puddings.

Traditional Desserts

07

Sticky Toffee Pudding

A heavenly, moist sponge cake made with finely chopped dates, completely smothered in a rich, warm, and buttery toffee sauce. Best served alongside a generous scoop of vanilla bean ice cream.

08

Apple Crumble

A comforting, rustic dessert composed of baked spiced apples sweetened with cinnamon, topped with a thick, crunchy layer of rub-in butter, flour, and brown sugar crumble texture.

09

Eton Mess

A delightfully chaotic summer dessert named after the famous Eton College. It consists of a crisp yet chewy broken meringue, fresh sweet strawberries, and pillows of softly whipped heavy cream folded together beautifully.
